# Brief Storyline Quality Standard

## Purpose

This file defines what good looks like for storyline work and what must be true before handoff.

Use it during drafting, challenge passes, and final validation.

## Core standard

- The storyline must answer a real decision question.
- Every major claim must be testable against evidence or flagged as a proof gap.
- Known, inferred, and unknown material must stay separate.
- Pillars must be distinct enough that they are not doing the same job twice.

## Authoring standard

- Build in stages: skeleton, bullets, short prose, flow pass, humanise pass, neutral-language pass.
- Keep the top-level answer and each pillar plain enough to survive executive skim-reading.
- Avoid recommendation language unless the brief explicitly requires recommendations.
- Remove decorative labels that make the logic sound stronger than it is.

## Tracking standard

- Keep the live baseline in `tracking/storyline-architecture.md`.
- Log proof gaps in `tracking/open-questions.md`.
- Log material tradeoffs and framing choices in `tracking/decision-log.md`.
- Do not leave rationale trapped in chat text.

## Handoff standard

- The artifact must be reviewable without oral explanation.
- Downstream storyboard or writing work must be able to inherit the current logic without guessing what the argument is meant to be.
- Any unresolved proof gap that could change the storyline must already be visible in tracking.
