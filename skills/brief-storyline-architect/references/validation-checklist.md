# Brief Storyline Validation Checklist

## Framing checks

- The governing question states the decision problem clearly.
- The intended audience is named.
- Scope and out-of-scope boundaries are explicit.
- The top-level answer responds directly to the governing question.

## Logic checks

- Each pillar has one clear claim.
- Pillars do not duplicate each other.
- Each pillar has an evidence path or an explicit proof gap.
- Objections and risks are recorded where they matter.
- The storyline can be read top to bottom without missing steps.

## Writing checks

- The artifact was built through skeleton, bullets, short prose, and flow passes.
- Language is neutral and simple.
- Humanisation removed machine habits without changing meaning.
- Consultant filler, vague verbs, and decorative slogans are gone.

## Tracking checks

- `tracking/storyline-architecture.md` is current.
- `tracking/open-questions.md` contains unresolved proof gaps.
- `tracking/decision-log.md` captures major framing choices.
